There's something about the first coffee of the morning that resists optimization. You can read about pour-over technique, debate single-origin vs blend, time the bloom to the second — and still, the moment that matters is the one before any of that. The kettle warming, the grinder humming, the quiet five minutes before anyone else is awake.

Maybe that's the point. Not the coffee itself, but a reliable place in the day where nothing is being asked of you.

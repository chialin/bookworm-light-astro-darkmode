The streets in this part of the city were paved before cars were a thing. They wind, narrow, and occasionally end without warning at someone's back door. Walking them is the kind of activity that resists a destination — you have to give in to wherever the street wants to take you.

![A narrow alley with hanging laundry](/images/posts/01.jpg)

## What I noticed

- Doors painted in colors no paint store sells today
- A cat that watched me pass without moving
- Three different smells of cooking from three different kitchens within a single block

## The pace of these places

Cities like this aren't slow exactly — there's traffic, errands, work. But the streets themselves slow you down. You can't drive through them. You can barely cycle. Your phone GPS gives up. The only honest way through is on foot, looking up.
